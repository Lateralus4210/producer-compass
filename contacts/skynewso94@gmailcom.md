## FL Studio Technical Setup
- **Template philosophy:** Has multiple templates — orchestral, rock/metal, synthwave, and per-game templates for projects using non-standard VSTs
- **Workhorse orchestral template:** ~150 channel rack instruments; uses all 125 FL mixer tracks
- **Instrument libraries layered:** Multiple libraries per section for strings, brass, and woodwinds; uses BBCSO as a starting/teaching baseline, with VSL and Heavyocity as the pro-tier additions
- **BRSO Articulate:** Central to his FL workflow — eliminates manual key switches, enables articulation switching by note color in piano roll, and provides expression/dynamics automation
- **Mixer routing pattern:** Individual instrument tracks → per-instrument reverb buses → orchestra reverb bus + percussion reverb bus (both with convolution reverb) → master channel (algorithmic reverb + spectrum analyzer + optional compressor/limiter)
- **Reverb approach:** Convolution reverb (Reverbary LE) on section buses to glue libraries together; algorithmic reverb (Ambience) on master to tie everything; intentionally avoids built-in library reverb so libraries can be swapped freely
- **Color coding in channel rack:** Strings = light sea green; brass = yellow; woodwinds = dark blue; harp/piano = green; percussion = beige; mallets = orange; plugin instances (BBCSO etc.) = grey/brown
- **Blank reserved tracks:** 10 open mixer tracks with blank Kontakt instances, pre-routed, for niche additions mid-session

## Five Keys to Music Production (~Oct/Nov 2025)
Skyler's core principles — published as a solo Video Vault piece, described by Zach as "best of the solo content."

1. **It's okay to be wrong** — Mistakes and criticism are inevitable and necessary. Extract lessons from both constructive and hostile criticism. Never say "I've learned it all." Musicianship is lifelong. Growth traceable across a catalog is endearing to listeners and instructive for yourself.
2. **Mindset over tools** — GAS (Gear Acquisition Syndrome) is the enemy. The best DAW/VST is the one you know how to use. Push free/budget tools to their limit first; only upgrade when you've genuinely hit the ceiling. Approach new tools from experience, not desperation.
3. **Tools are an investment** — Buying pro tools is a vote of confidence in yourself. "Skin in the game." Reframe purchases as a promise to make the tool pay for itself. Having everything via piracy was its own form of GAS — paralysis from too many options. Buying one at a time forces commitment and learning.
4. **Deadlines are king** — Game jams gave Skyler the discipline to finish. "Good enough" beats "perfect and unfinished." Real deadlines (game jams, collaborators) or artificial ones (Friday midnight) both work. 3,000 hours + 200 tracks > 3,000 hours + 5 tracks. Only move forward, never revisit.
5. **Release everything** — Music on a hard drive does no good. Imperfection is human. Releasing is another opportunity to be wrong and learn. Fans are out there waiting — don't keep them waiting. Trace your growth through your catalog.
