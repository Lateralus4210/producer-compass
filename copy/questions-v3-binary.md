# Compass Skill Tree — Questions V3 (Binary Yes/No)
_Calibrated for stuck producers (2–5+ years in, inconsistent output, not breaking through)._
_Not a beginner diagnostic. A beginner gets mostly No. A stuck producer gets a mix._
_Category sequence: Awareness · Avoidance · Practice · Consistency · Intentionality · Output · Reflection_

---

## 0. Ideation
_(Curriculum: active listening, idea capture system, genre exploration, starting with a direction, idea backlog)_

| # | Category | Question |
|---|----------|----------|
| 1 | Awareness | I know the specific thing that kills my creative momentum — and it's not just "not being in the mood." |
| 2 | Avoidance | I start new track ideas even when I don't feel inspired. |
| 3 | Practice | I listen to music outside my usual genres at least once a week — intentionally, not by accident. |
| 4 | Consistency | I have a system for capturing ideas that I actually use — voice memos, a notebook, quick DAW sketches — not just my memory. |
| 5 | Intentionality | Before opening my DAW, I usually have a starting point: a reference, a concept, a constraint, or a sound I want to explore. |
| 6 | Output | I've started at least 3 new track ideas in the last 30 days — not just revisited old ones. |
| 7 | Reflection | I've listened back to a finished track and noted specifically what creative choices worked and why. |

## 1. Composition
_(Curriculum: song structure, tension/release, arrangement decisions, variation/dynamics, listener engagement)_

| # | Category | Question |
|---|----------|----------|
| 1 | Awareness | I understand the difference between composition (what the parts are) and arrangement (what enters/exits and when). |
| 2 | Avoidance | I push tracks through to a full arrangement even when the middle section feels stuck or unclear. |
| 3 | Practice | I've actively studied how a track I admire builds and releases tension over its full runtime — not just enjoyed it. |
| 4 | Consistency | Most of my tracks have a clear beginning, middle, and end — not just looping sections with no arc. |
| 5 | Intentionality | I make deliberate decisions about when elements enter and exit — I can explain why a section hits the way it does. |
| 6 | Output | I've finished at least 2 fully arranged tracks (not just loops or sketches) in the last 6 months. |
| 7 | Reflection | I've compared the energy arc of one of my arrangements against a reference track — not just the sounds. |

| # | Category | Question |
|---|----------|----------|
| 1 | Awareness | I know what key my tracks are in before I mix them. |
| 2 | Avoidance | I engage with theory concepts even when they slow me down or feel academic. |

## 3. DAW
_(Curriculum: workflow efficiency, keyboard shortcuts, session organization, routing/busing, session template)_

| # | Category | Question |
|---|----------|----------|
| 1 | Awareness | I can name the specific moments in a session where I lose creative momentum to technical friction. |
| 2 | Avoidance | I work through technical issues in my DAW rather than abandoning the session or saving it for later. |
| 3 | Practice | I use keyboard shortcuts for at least 5 common actions — without stopping to think about it. |
| 4 | Consistency | I use a session template that reflects how I actually work — not just the DAW default or someone else's setup. |
| 5 | Intentionality | My sessions are organized — named tracks, clean routing, logical structure — not just when I'm being careful. |
| 6 | Output | I can go from a blank session to a workable rough idea in under 30 minutes. |
| 7 | Reflection | In the last 3 months, I've identified and changed at least one real inefficiency in my workflow. |

## 4. Mixing
_(Curriculum: gain staging, EQ for clarity, compression, reference tracks, translation checking, mix readiness criteria)_

| # | Category | Question |
|---|----------|----------|
| 1 | Awareness | I understand that mixing is solving for translation and clarity — not just making it sound louder or fuller. |
| 2 | Avoidance | I mix my tracks through to completion even when I'm not confident about the outcome. |
| 3 | Practice | I start every mix with gain staging before touching EQ or compression. |
| 4 | Consistency | I check my mixes on at least 2 different playback systems before calling them done. |
| 5 | Intentionality | I load a reference track at the start of every mix and compare to it actively — not just at the end. |
| 6 | Output | In the last 6 months, someone gave me positive feedback specifically on the sound quality of a mix — not just the music itself. |
| 7 | Reflection | I've A/B'd one of my mixes against a professional reference at matched loudness. |

## 5. Mastering
_(Curriculum: mastering vs. mixing distinction, LUFS targets, headroom, translation checking, self-master vs. outsource decision)_

| # | Category | Question |
|---|----------|----------|
| 1 | Awareness | I understand what mastering is responsible for as distinct from mixing — and it's not just adding a limiter. |
| 2 | Avoidance | Every track I release has gone through an intentional mastering process — not just a quick limiter pass. |
| 3 | Practice | I know the target LUFS for the platforms I release on and hit it intentionally, not by feel. |
| 4 | Consistency | I leave sufficient headroom in my mix before mastering — I'm not maxing out the mix bus. |
| 5 | Intentionality | I decide whether to self-master or outsource based on the project — not just out of habit or cost avoidance. |
| 6 | Output | My last 3 releases translated well across earbuds, laptop speakers, and a car — without needing a second pass. |
| 7 | Reflection | I've A/B'd one of my masters against a professional reference at matched loudness — not just compared impressions. |

## Notes

- **Scoring**: Yes = 1 pt, No = 0 pt. 7 pts max per area → map to 1–10.
- **Calibration target**: A stuck producer (2–5+ years, serious, inconsistent output) should get a meaningful mix of Yes and No — not mostly Yes, not mostly No. Questions that most stuck producers answer No to are the most diagnostic.
- **Avoidance questions** (Q2) are framed positively — Yes = not avoiding. Diagnostic value comes from which questions score No, not total score alone.
- **Output questions** (Q6) use specific timeframes and counts — these are the hardest to rationalize. They're intentionally concrete.
- **Awareness questions** (Q1) are recalibrated from the previous version — they're not asking "do you know what X is" but "do you have specific situational knowledge about your own practice."
