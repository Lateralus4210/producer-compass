# Compass Skill Tree — Post-Area Whisper Lines

These display as a conditional modal after an area completes, triggered only when that area's score falls below a threshold (tentatively < 5/10). One line per area. Voice: coaching observation, not advice or pitch.

Last updated: 2026-04-06

---

**Composition**
"We find that producers who struggle with composition don't realize the muscle is in the finishing, not the starting — a weak idea you complete is worth more than a great one you abandon."

**Theory**
"We find that producers who struggle with theory are already using it — they just don't have words for what they're doing yet. The vocabulary comes after the ear, not before."

**DAW**
"We find that producers who struggle with their DAW are fighting a workflow problem, not a knowledge problem — the goal isn't knowing more shortcuts, it's removing the gap between idea and sound."

**Mixing**
"We find that producers who struggle with mixing don't realize it's about making a song sound more like itself, not rewriting a song."

**Mastering**
"We find that producers who struggle with mastering are trying to fix the mix — mastering can't save a bad mix, and a good mix barely needs mastering."

**Collaboration**
"We find that producers who struggle with collaboration haven't clearly defined their own sound yet — you can't share a vision you haven't articulated."

**Artwork/Content**
"We find that producers who struggle with artwork are making a design decision when they should be making a listening decision — the best covers tell you what the music feels like before you press play."

**Release**
"We find that producers who struggle with releasing never decided what 'done' means — they're not stuck on the music, they're stuck on a definition."

**Ideation**
"We find that producers who score low on ideation don't have an idea problem — they have a selection problem. Too many unfinished ideas usually means no system for choosing which one deserves to get finished."

**Promo**
"We find that producers who struggle with promo haven't decided who they're talking to — so everything they post goes everywhere and lands nowhere."
