# Questions V2 — Statement / Self-Assessment Format

**Format:** Each item is a first-person statement. User responds on a 4-point slider:
`Not me → Kind of → Mostly → That's me` (1–4 pts)

**Calibration intent:**
- Ideation, DAW: medium scores expected (accessible but not trivial)
- Composition, Theory, Mixing: medium-hard (wide variance)
- Mastering, Collab, Artwork: medium-low (often neglected)
- Release, Promo: lowest scores expected (the coaching hook)

---

## 0. Ideation

1. I regularly listen to music I wouldn't normally make.
2. When inspiration hits, I capture it before it disappears.
3. I know what kind of track I want to make before I open my DAW.
4. My influences are wider than my output.
5. I study tracks I admire, not just enjoy them.
6. I finish more ideas than I abandon.
7. My creative well doesn't run dry.

---

## 1. Composition

1. My tracks have a clear emotional arc from start to finish.
2. I know exactly why each section of my arrangement exists.
3. I use contrast and dynamics intentionally, not just by feel.
4. My transitions feel inevitable, not just adequate.
5. Listeners who don't make music tell me my tracks keep them engaged.
6. I can describe the structure of any track I've made.
7. I regularly finish arrangements I'm genuinely proud of.

---

## 2. Theory

1. I can figure out the key of a track just by listening.
2. I know why a chord progression creates the feeling it does.
3. I use theory to make decisions, not just explain them after the fact.
4. I'm comfortable working in multiple keys and scales.
5. I understand how rhythm and timing create feel and groove.
6. I can communicate a musical idea to another musician without humming it.
7. Theory expands what I can make, not just what I can explain.

---

## 3. DAW

1. My DAW gets out of the way when I'm in a creative flow.
2. My shortcuts are muscle memory — I rarely use menus.
3. I can diagnose a technical problem without derailing a session.
4. I understand signal flow and routing well enough to explain it.
5. My projects are organized enough that I could hand one off to someone else.
6. I use synthesis and sound design, not just presets.
7. Recording live audio or vocals is a smooth part of my workflow.

---

## 4. Mixing

1. I reference professional tracks throughout my mix, not just at the end.
2. My mix decisions are intentional — I know why I'm doing what I'm doing.
3. I can hear a frequency problem and know exactly how to fix it.
4. My low end is tight and controlled on every system I check.
5. I always know what my mix sounds like in mono.
6. Compression is a creative tool for me, not just damage control.
7. My mixes translate — they hold up outside my studio.

---

## 5. Mastering

1. I understand what mastering actually does versus what mixing does.
2. I have a target loudness in mind for each platform before I export.
3. I check every master on at least three different playback systems.
4. I know when to master myself and when to hire someone.
5. The headroom in my masters is intentional, not accidental.
6. My releases sound competitive next to professionally mastered music.
7. Mastering is a deliberate step in my process, not an afterthought.

---

## 6. Collab

1. I regularly share unfinished work with other producers for feedback.
2. The feedback I give is specific and useful, not just encouraging.
3. I've reached out to start a collaboration in the past few months.
4. I know how to protect my rights when working with someone else.
5. I'm part of a community that actively makes me a better producer.
6. I can receive hard feedback without getting defensive.
7. Collaboration has meaningfully changed how I make music.

---

## 7. Artwork

1. My visual identity is as intentional as my sound.
2. Someone who doesn't know me could recognize my content at a glance.
3. I create content consistently, not just when a release forces it.
4. I know what to post and when — I don't just wing it.
5. My cover art and visuals match the energy of my music.
6. I think about what I write in captions, not just what I post.
7. My visual presence actively helps me grow.

---

## 8. Release

1. I release music on a consistent, predictable schedule.
2. I pitch my music to playlists before it comes out, not after.
3. Every release has a plan behind it, not just an upload date.
4. I understand metadata, ISRC codes, and distribution logistics cold.
5. I promote releases before, during, and after they drop.
6. I look at my stream data and use it to make better decisions next time.
7. I know exactly how my last release performed and why.

---

## 9. Promo

1. I have a clear picture of who my music is actually for.
2. I'm actively building an audience outside of streaming platforms.
3. I have more than one way to make money from my music.
4. When I spend money on my music, I know what return I'm looking for.
5. I track my music income and expenses.
6. I understand what a paid advertising campaign for my music would look like.
7. My music career has a financial plan, not just a creative vision.

---

## Notes

- In this format, the `a:` array in the HTML is replaced by fixed slider labels: `["That's me","Mostly","Kind of","Not me"]` (best→worst, matching the existing 4pt scoring)
- Flavor text per dot position can be added per-statement in a future pass
